Questions

Fractional CMO, answered.

What is a fractional CMO?

A senior marketing leader who runs your marketing part-time and on a flexible basis, instead of as a full-time hire. You get CMO-level strategy and execution without the salary, the equity, or the long search. It fits B2B companies past product-market fit that need senior thinking before they can justify a full-time CMO.

What's the difference between a CMO and a fractional CMO?

Same seniority, different commitment. A CMO is a full-time executive on your payroll. A fractional CMO brings the same strategic depth part-time or by project, embedded in your team but without the permanent overhead. For many growing companies, fractional is how they get CMO-calibre leadership years before a full-time role makes sense.

What does a fractional CMO cost?

Far less than a full-time CMO's salary, benefits, and equity, because you pay only for the time you need. Engagements range from a two-week audit to a multi-month embedded retainer, so the cost scales to the scope. The point is senior leadership you can access now, sized to where the business actually is.

When should you hire a fractional CMO?

When you have product-market fit but marketing is still figuring itself out, or when you have a capable team that needs senior direction during a strategic moment. If you are carrying the marketing gap yourself, or your CMO has no time to think or build, that is the signal.

What is an AI marketing system?

The layer above the tools: shared context, workflows, and measurement that make AI actually move the business instead of sitting in individual chat windows. I build these so they run inside your team and keep working after the engagement ends.

How is your approach different from a traditional fractional CMO?

Most fractional CMOs deliver strategy and team coaching. I do that, and I also build the AI marketing systems that keep running after the engagement ends: shared context, automated workflows, measurement layers your team owns. The diagnosis earns the cure, but the cure is built, not just recommended. That is the wedge: a CMO who codes the systems, not just the slides.
